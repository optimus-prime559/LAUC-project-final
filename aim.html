⦿ Aim of the Experiment

This virtual laboratory experiment aims to help engineering students understand and visualize the fundamental principles of heat conduction through different geometries, specifically plane walls and cylindrical pipes, as described by Fourier’s Law of Heat Conduction.

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand the mechanism of heat conduction in solids.
  • Comprehend Fourier’s Law and its mathematical formulation for different geometries.
  • Analyze heat conduction through plane walls and cylindrical pipes.
  • Calculate heat transfer rates for insulated pipes.
  • Understand radial heat flow in cylindrical coordinates.
  • Explore practical applications in engineering systems.

By the end of this experiment, students should be able to predict heat transfer rates through different materials and geometries, and understand factors affecting thermal conduction in applications such as building insulation, heat exchangers, and pipeline systems.
